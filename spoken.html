Author: [Nikolett Mus]

Date: [28.05.2025]

1. Introduction

This document is a draft outlining the techniques, considerations, and conventions used in annotating spoken language-specific features, such as prosodic tags, discourse-related phenomena, and structural relations, in the Tundra Nenets data included in the first release of the Nenets UD Treebank. It reflects the preliminary practices adopted during the annotation process and serves as a reference for ongoing development and refinement.

2. Spoken Phenomena: Tags and Relational Encoding

  1. Noises

    TAG:

    • <n_n> = Non-linguistic noise (speaker-generated): cough, laugh, sigh
    • <w_n> = Environmental/world noise: background chatter, traffic, mic bump

    ID: yes

    FORM: tag

    LEMMA: tag

    UPOS: X

    DEPREL: noise (attached to the element it follows)

    Gloss: NOISE

  2. Speech Interruptions

    Interruptions in the speaker's normal flow, including errors, hesitations, and restarts.

    1.1 Disfluency

    • <a_d> = audible disfluency (e.g., "ahm", "uh", "erm")
      ID: lexical unit on its own
      FORM: <a_d>
      LEMMA: <a_d>
      UPOS: INTJ (interjection)
      DEPREL: discourse, attached to the adjacent (following) word
      Gloss: DISFL
    • <a> = articulatory lengthening (hesitation by prolongation)
      ID: not relevant
      FORM: Attached to the relevant lexeme without space
      LEMMA: NO
      UPOS: not relevant
      DEPREL: not relevant
      Gloss: not relevant

    1.2 Cut-offs

    • <u_l> = unfinished lexeme
      ID: not relevant
      FORM: Attached to the lexeme without space
      LEMMA: full expected word
      UPOS: not relevant
      DEPREL: not relevant
      Gloss: not relevant

    1.3 False Start

    • <f_s> = false start
      ID: not relevant
      FORM: Attached to the lexeme without space: <f_s>word
      LEMMA: underlying lemma (without tag)
      UPOS: POS of base word
      DEPREL: reparandum; attached to the node that repairs it
      Gloss: gloss of the intended word

    1.4 Repetition

    • <e_r> = exact repetition
      ID: not relevant
      FORM: Attached to the lexeme: <e_r>word
      LEMMA: underlying lemma (without tag)
      UPOS: POS of base word
      DEPREL: reparandum; attached to the next instance
      Gloss: gloss of base/intended word
    • <p_r> = partial repetition
      ID: not relevant
      FORM: Attached to the lexeme: <p_r>word
      LEMMA: underlying lemma (without tag)
      UPOS: POS of base word
      DEPREL: reparandum; attached to the next instance
      Gloss: gloss of base/intended word
  1. Connected Speech

    Phenomena where word boundaries become less distinct due to natural speech flow:

    • <m_l> = merged lexemes
      ID: not relevant
      FORM: Lexemes must be separated; tag <m_l> precedes the next lexeme without space (e.g., сюрте <m_l>хэя)
      LEMMA: underlying lemma (without the tag)
      UPOS: POS of the lexeme
      DEPREL: As per syntax — based on the lexeme’s role
      Gloss: gloss of the lemma

    Note: This also includes sandhi effects (sound changes across word boundaries).

  2. Lexical Error

    Incorrect word use:

    • <u_w> = unusual word (word choice error)
      ID: not relevant
      FORM: Tag <u_w> attached directly to the lexeme (e.g., <u_w>word)
      LEMMA: lemma of the incorrect word (without the tag)
      UPOS: POS of the incorrect word
      DEPREL: syntactic function as if correct
      Gloss: literal gloss of the incorrect word
  3. Pauses
    • <s_p> = silent pause (no vocalization for a measurable duration)
    • <b_p> = breathing pause

    a) Structural Pauses

    • Occur at clear syntactic boundaries (e.g., end of main/embedded clauses, sentence-final)
      ID: yes
      FORM: tag
      LEMMA: tag
      UPOS: PUNCT
      DEPREL: PUNCT — attached to the head of the clause/sentence it follows
      Note: The final punctuation always depends on the root
      Gloss: SIL

    b) Disfluency-Related Pauses

    • Occur inside phrases or between constituents, not marking structure
      ID: yes
      FORM: tag
      LEMMA: tag
      UPOS: X
      DEPREL: disfluency — attached to the element it follows (e.g., <a_d>)
      Gloss: PAUSE
