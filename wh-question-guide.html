Syntactic Diagnostics for Wh-Questions in Underdescribed SOV Languages

Author: [Nikolett Mus]

Preamble

This questionnaire is designed to support the investigation of the syntactic structure of wh-questions in SOV languages, particularly those whose interrogative constructions have received little to no detailed syntactic description to date.

Rather than offering a comprehensive analysis, the document functions as a practical orientation tool, particularly in cases where the syntactic configuration of wh-questions appears, at least superficially, to mirror that of declarative ones.

Among the central issues in the study of wh-questions is the syntactic position of wh-expressions. Accordingly, the present guide places primary emphasis on this aspect.

The central aim is to help to identify and elucidate the potential factors, most notably, the morphosyntactic and semantic properties of wh-elements, that may condition/influence their positional behavior within the clause.

1. Part-of-Speech and Phrase-Level Function of the Wh-Phrase

This parameter concerns the grammatical category and syntactic behavior of the wh-expression, considered at the level of the phrase in which it appears. The goal is to determine the morphosyntactic profile of the wh-phrase in the language under investigation.

  • Morphological distribution:b> This aspect focuses on the morphological properties of the wh-words. Specifically, it examines the types of affixes (both inflectional and derivational) that the wh-element can or must carry. This includes number and case marking, agreement morphology, and any other relevant morphological features that may be added to a wh-word.
  • Syntactic distribution:b> This refers to the range of syntactic environments in which the wh-word can occur. It involves identifying the phrase's co-occurrence patterns and syntactic dependencies: for instance, what kinds of modifiers it can take (e.g., adjectives, determiners), what elements it can modify (e.g., nouns, verbs).
